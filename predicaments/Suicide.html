The Bhagavad Gita

Suicide is the deliberate killing of oneself. Beware the silent solace and solitude of those seagrass meadows swaying like manes under the frosted surface of the rivers of death.

You are but a droplet in a waterfall, don't rush the remerge, I'll meet you again somewhere, there is a house on an island, I think I saw laundry swaying too, in the wind, on a string tied between the porch and the oak, you know that oak, there was a cat warm purring slow tail, it surely lived, I smell coffee, meet me there, we can talk, or if nothing else, there is a patch under the oak, a patch of moss, it has your prints on it, don't go just yet.
