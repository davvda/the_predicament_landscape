Language

The Potawatomi language consists of around 70% verbs compared to 30% in the English language. Why would that matter? The world of the Potawatomi is alive, being, doing, becoming. The world of English consists mostly of static things, inanimate objects. There is a lot to language, especially since the language we learn as children tends to be the language that structures and leads our thoughts for the rest of our lives. It is also nearly impossible to escape the frames of one's language in imagination and thinking processes.
