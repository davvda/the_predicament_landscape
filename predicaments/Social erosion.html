Social erosion

Social erosion, or social cohesion erosion, is the gnawing away and [crumbling of civil society fractured by divergent forces from within](https://www.fastcompany.com/90712209/why-global-leaders-are-terrified-about-social-cohesion-erosion). According to a WEF report it is the risk that has worsened the most since COVID-19. Social cohesion generally means a sense of togetherness, trust, opportunities, and orientation towards a common goal. This erosion, similarly to soil erosion, occurs when society is ruled carelessly with incoherent short-term priorities and undue force. Just as soil erosion risks us losing all fertile soil, social erosion risks us losing our society.
