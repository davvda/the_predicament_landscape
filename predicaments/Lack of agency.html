Lack of agency

How wonderful it is that nobody needs to wait a single moment before starting to improve the world.

Anne Frank

Agency is the capacity to act, to effectively set in motion, powerfully incite action. From the smallest scale of single human decisions to decisions on planetary scale, we often seem to lack agency or, at the very least, lack a belief in our agency. Agency is also, more specifically, the capacity to do something differently from, or in addition to, the events that simply happen to you. It is, in other words, independent from the direct context and events of your situation. You can always increase your agency in some way, find how and where you're capable of action.

The secret of the world is that it is a very malleable place, we must be sure that people learn this, and never forget the order: Learning is naturally the consequence of doing.

Simon Sarris

If nothing else, learning to accept situations where you experience a lack agency can lead to an internal agency of thought that suddenly changes everything. There are no moments that completely lack agency, you just have to notice it.
