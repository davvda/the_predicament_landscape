Lack of attentiveness

Being attentive means being observant, seeing changes in one's surroundings, noticing the needs and wants of others, being in service to others. To lack attentiveness is to not notice the effects of one's actions, the needs and wants of others, both human and non-human, to not be observant to changes in one's surroundings, to lack the care and tending to that the world requires from us.

Outgoing connections:

  • Delusional domination of nature
  • Ignorance of interconnectedness
  • Individualism
  • Illusion of understanding
  • Sense-making crisis
  • Knowledge isn't wisdom
  • Nothing is local
  • Cognitive dissonance
