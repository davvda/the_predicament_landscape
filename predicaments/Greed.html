Greed

So the unwanting soul
sees what's hidden,
and the ever-wanting soul
sees only what it wants.

Lao Tzu

Greed is complicated. It's often defined as the excessively eager desire to possessand is commonly talked about as one of the drivers of capitalism, the basic fundament of human nature. I would argue that greed is a response to fear of scarcity, either in the present moment or projected into the future. Capitalism rewards accumulation, drives fear of scarcity, and generates and maintains greed.

Be fearful when others are greedy and greedy when others are fearful.

Warren Buffett
