Violence

What is violence? What you think of is probably physical violence - causing bodily harm to someone. Violence is any path that uses undue force. Now, does any wise path actually require any force at all? There is violence in thought, words, and actions. To end violence, therefore, we must first find peace in our thoughts and perceptions.

The two most clear ones are that cognitive dissonance often becomes a requirement for the wielder of violence when the victim's ability to fight back is low. The cognitive dissonance required to believe one to be a good person while throwing a baby out of a window and raping and killing the mother, as an example. The other is the cognitive dissonance required by both the perpetrator and the civilians on the perpetrator's side that justifies that violence. Just think for a moment... The other day I heard someone at my local gym scoff at the fact that there were protestors outside their university because a Swedish weapons manufacturer was there at a recruiting event. Now, this person is a fully reasonable person, relatively, and was fully entrenched in the opinion of the stupidity of these protestors. Meanwhile, clueless engineers mingle with students to find those most violently intelligent to work on efficient technology for killing humans.
