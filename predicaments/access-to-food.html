Access to food

Access to food means having access to the necessary energy and nutrients to live. This means living in a place where one can grow fruits and vegetables, one can hunt or fish, raise livestock, forage and gather wild plants and fungi, or have food transported to one's location.

When I give food to the poor, they call me a saint. When I ask why the poor have no food, they call me a communist.

Dom Helder Camara

Outgoing connections:

  • Access to water - Pretty clear connection. To grow food you need water. Also, there are people living close to thirsty avocado farms who don't always have access to neither water nor avocados.
  • Addiction - The constant ease by which we can reach out for sugar and fat, historically connected to nutrient-dense, hard-to-find food, leads to addictive behavior with regards to our eating habits.
  • Agriculture - Food being or not being accessible for certain people or places is one of the main drivers of modern agriculture and all it entails.
  • Ancient sunlight, old sunlight, current sunlight - The energy in the food we grow and eat consists of old sunlight in varying amounts, usually captured, produced, manufactured, and harvested with the energy of ancient sunlight.
  • Animal views - Many connections here. Here's one. The ease of access to packaged flesh has made many of us mostly unaware or at the very least unthinking, unfeeling about who the flesh comes from.
  • Biodiversity loss - Most of the previously biodiverse places on Earth are now plantations.
  • Centralisation - The majority of the people of modernity depend on a select few producers to ensure their access to food.
  • Cognitive dissonance - How many contemplate where/what/who their food comes from?
  • Colonialist narratives - Are any of those who grow the coffee I drink whiter or wealthier than me?
  • Consumerism - If it's there, right in front of you, grab it, eat it. Oreo trees were rare on the savannah, y'know.
  • Cost of living - One's access to food has clear connections to one's cost of living. Perhaps Russia should leave Ukraine alone simply to lower the price of wheat in Europe?

  • Despair - Places which, either suddenly or gradually, lose their access to food rapidly turn to despair when actions fail to remedy the decline.
  • Ecological crisis - Access to food and our means of getting it is a main driver of our ecological crisis.
  • Energy - Food directly gives us energy to live.

  • Hedonism - Food is one of life's greatest pleasures. It's also easy to overindulge when the accessible amounts of it seem infinite.
