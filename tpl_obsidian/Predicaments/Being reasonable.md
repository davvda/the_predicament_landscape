**To be reasonable is, or rather should be, to think through and to understand**. Most features of our predicament landscape have arisen due to our commitment to (try to) be reasonable. Unreasonably reasonable. To stop deforestation is not seen as reasonable, to [cut down most of the old growth forests](https://theconversation.com/sweden-has-vast-old-growth-forests-but-they-are-being-chopped-down-faster-than-the-amazon-218753) apparently is. Reasoning has its merits but it seems we must dare to be a bit unreasonable to be able to manage our modern predicaments. All reasoning tends to follow a certain optimisation and prioritisation and if we haven't figured out what to optimise for (meta-optimisation) our reasoning has little value.
