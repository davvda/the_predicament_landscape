Energy inefficiencies

In the U.S. approximately 7 calories are used to produce 1 calorie of food energy. Mostly fossil fuels for machines used for plowing, planting, harvesting, sorting, etc. This ratio combined with the exosomatic energy use presents a picture of a highly inefficient food system. For a civilisation obsessed with inefficiency we sure suck at it. Perhaps surprisingly, this is the case for more than just food. The foundational fault may be our lack of understanding adequacy and what we need, spending precious fuels on things we want and needlessly desire. I am uncertain about the weight of this predicament but it is important to have included in the landscape our inability to efficiently extract and use energy.
