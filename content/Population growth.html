Population growth


The world population of humans has increased from around 1 billion in 1800 to 8 billion in 2024. This increase has mainly been driven by fossil fuels and technologies that have increased the amount of usable energy. Important to note is that the growth rate peaked in 1963 and has since plummeted, now projecting a maximum population of around 10 billion some time after the year 2050.

Outgoing connections:

  • Access to food - More mouths to feed.

  • Architecture - Housing, housing, housing. The ugliest buildings in Sweden were built during the "miljonprojektet" in the 60's, whose purpose was to quickly build housing for a million people. Soviet brick stacks and concrete boxes.
  • Ecological crisis - Don't misunderstand me. 10 billion people could live sustainably on the planet. The way an average human lives currently, however, makes population growth a massive ecological issue. Even more so considering the pace of growth, around 1 billion in 1800 and 10 billion by 2060.
  • Educational systems - To what extent do educational systems work the way they do for the simple reason that they must accomodate an increasing amount of students?
  • Energy - All humans need energy. Heating, eating, cleaning, cooking, driving, entertainment, lighting, communication, etc, etc.

Don't get me wrong, I'm not too keen on mass starvation, I'm just not sure population growth is a sign of progress in any meaningful way. That which allows population growth to occur may be related to real progress but I am left wondering if we once again mistake certain proxies for the key metrics in question.
