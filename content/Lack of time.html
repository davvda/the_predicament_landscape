Lack of time

In philosophy if you aren't moving at a snail's pace you aren't moving at all.

- Iris Murdoch

We lack time in a multitude of ways. I would, however, separate our lack of time into two categories, one being duration and the other being the prioritisation of the present moment. There is a feeling of impending doom in many people, that there isn't enough time to course correct, to halt global warming, to stop the machine before it kills us. The future is shrinking, we lack continuance of time, instead calcifying our hearts awaiting the crash with a hardened future. The second category, the prioritisation of the present, is what makes the changes that are required even less likely to happen. We, as individuals, communities, societies, nations, don't have time to spend on slowing down or halting certain activities while doubling down on others. We don't have time to not be employed, not grow the economy, travel, provide, we don't have time to not destroy the world. If we must spend our time destroying it, how can we even attempt to prioritise saving it at any moment?

Of these two categories, I would implore you to ignore the former and focus on the latter. Eternity does not lie in duration, it lies in the infinitely complex web of the moment. To find a way forward, every step of every moment has to be radical and daring.

It's too late to be ready.

- Dogen Zenji
