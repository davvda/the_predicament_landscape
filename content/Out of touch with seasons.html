Out of touch with seasons

Modernity doesn't do well with natural cycles. The seasons are often, quite arbitrarily, divided into four: winter, spring, summer, autumn. The Japanese have a system of 72 micro-seasons and most cultures have undoubtedly found importance in the two solstices and equinoxes, the arrival of certain birds, the changing of colours, the shapes of clouds, the spirit of the wind, the singing of frogs, the position of the stars, harvest, ripening of fruit, decay of leaves, temperature of the soil on bare feet, vigour of rainfall, texture of snow, lightness of time, texture of the air, migration of deer, habits of rabbits, aging, and death.
