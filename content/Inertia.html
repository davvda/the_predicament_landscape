Inertia

Inertia is the tendency of objects in motion to stay in motion and objects at rest to stay at rest. As a predicament, inertia is the unbudging nature of the trendlines and modes of being of modern civilisation. Through energy extraction and economic growth we've accelerated to such speeds that we seem unable to slow down and, if the engine was to stop, our inertia would keep us heading straight for the precipice. Inertia presents us with the fact that it wouldn't be enough to stop our negatively impacting actions, we would have to actively reverse their effects or prepare to recover from the crash. A controlled descent, a hospicing of modernity.
