Lack of feeling needed

You are needed. At the very least, if you disagree, you are needed in the sense that you are a part of the world and with the changing of the world there will be a change of you. What you do with and about this change is up to you. Modernity has, however, created the predicament that few, if any, feel needed. Maybe there is, at the very ground of Maslow's hierarchy of needs, the need to feel needed.

Outgoing connections:

  • Apathy - If you have difficulty feeling like you're a necessary part of the world, you won't care.
  • Bullshit jobs - If you aren't needed you might just as well waste your life on menial crap. It doesn't matter, does it?
  • Carelessness
  • Complexity mismatch - Imagine the arising complexity of actions if everyone suddenly realised they could contribute to the good?
  • Democracy failing - When they need you, they'll call you.
  • Despair
  • Directionlessness - Nothing makes the direction of required action more clear than being and feeling needed.
  • Distractions - Distracted from our perceived emptiness and purposelessness.
  • Emotional crisis
  • Failure to take responsibility - Most arguments against taking responsibility seems to be that we somehow can't? Or shouldn't? Or that it won't matter? Weak lives.
  • Hedonism - Nothing remains but meaningless pleasure-seeking.
  • Identity - If you aren't anything to anyone else, who are you?
  • Ignorance of interconnectedness - Whatever you do will have some effect.
  • Ignoring diversity - Whatever you do, whatever *you* do might be what's missing.
  • Individualism
  • Lack of belonging
  • Lack of purpose
  • Loneliness
  • Loss of cultural complexity - Imagine the creativity of solutions and interventions if people felt truly needed for the challenges of modernity.
  • Meaning crisis - What greater meaning than being wanted and needed?
  • Mental health - In the mist of my teenage depressive amnesia I can still vividly remember helping my grandfather removing snow from his log cabin up north.
  • Selfishness - At least I need me...
  • Sex and sexuality
  • Sexism - Women and men seem to have no clue.
  • The death of creative work - Especially now that AI-models can generate whatever commercial crap we consume.
  • Unemployment - Sadly, the peak of feeling needed often comes in the form of employment. I'm sorry if that's all you feel.
  • Unpaid important labour - Perhaps needed but unrewarded and not prioritised?
