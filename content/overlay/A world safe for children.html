A world safe for children

It can sometimes be useful, when pondering how things might be different, to stick to one single criterium. What would a world safe for children look like? Take a moment and reflect, perhaps on the risks and dangers of your own childhood, your own children, the children of friends, family, and acquaintances, children in other places, perhaps children in other times. Traffic, criminal paths, bullying, slavery, domestic abuse, violence, sexual exploitation, school, navigating the unguided transition to adulthood, puberty, sex, access to play, social media, screens, stressed parents, absent parents, ultra-processed food, I'm sure one could continue for quite some time. However, we're not interested in making the world artificially risk-free and absent of danger. We're simply interested in making childhood safer. It is, after all, in childhood that the adult human takes shape. It takes a village to raise a child. What does it take to ensure a child's safety?
