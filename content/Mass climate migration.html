Mass climate migration

A case, and quite a convincing one at that, can be made that the majority of migrations of the past few hundred years have been related to climate change.

The Syrian refugee crisis saw around a million Syrians coming to Europe. This created enormous political tension, increased racism, furthering of right extremism, and is still seen as the major destabilising event in countries that decided to host a large portion of Syrian refugees, like Germany and Sweden. By 2050 it is estimated that at least 1.2 billion people could be displaced by climate-related events. If humanity fails to coordinate around climate migration or, at the very least, navigate the chaotic inclusion of those whose displacement we've caused, unprecedented disaster awaits.

  • Climate footprints - As people wander from one place to another because of climate change, the size of their footprints will get larger and larger. Why? Simple. The countries most vulnerable to current climate change are poor countries with high population densities. People tend to flee towards wealthy countries with lower population densities. These wealthier countries have much larger individual climate footprints. This connection isn't necessarily a truthful one but there's still something to it.
