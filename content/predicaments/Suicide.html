Suicide

Our mistake is in taking this for ultimate reality, like the dreamer thinking that nothing is real except his dream.

- The Bhagavad Gita

Suicide is the deliberate killing of oneself. Beware the silent solace and solitude of those seagrass meadows swaying like manes under the frosted surface of the rivers of death.

You are but a droplet in a waterfall, don't rush the remerge, I'll meet you again somewhere, there is a house on an island, I think I saw laundry swaying too, in the wind, on a string tied between the porch and the oak, you know that oak, there was a cat warm purring slow tail, alive, I smell coffee, meet me there, we can talk, or, if nothing else, there is a patch under the oak, of moss, it has your prints on it, don't go just yet.

Outgoing connections:

  • Avoiding radical options - Suicide might seem to be the radical option. Often, for the suicidal, it feels like the only viable option.
  • Avoiding uncertainty - Certain death over uncertain life.
  • Dealing with death
  • Emotional crisis - For those remaining.
  • Failure to take responsibility - Much left undone and unspoken.
  • Illusion of no option
  • Loss of cultural complexity - What's been lost?
  • Naive progress narrative - Finland is often talked about as the happiest country on Earth. It also has the highest suicide rate of any Nordic country. Fortunately, in other words, that the unhappy people remove themselves from the data.
