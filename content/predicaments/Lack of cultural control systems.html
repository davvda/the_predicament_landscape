Lack of cultural control systems

Wisdom, spirituality, animism, and elders have contributed historically to creating cultural control systems which, as opposed to laws, control behaviours with an open hand. Modernity mostly lacks cultural control systems as these have been replaced by game theory. When people mention the need for religion, "a new christianity", or a complete destruction of capitalism, I believe the foundational discussion that must be had is one of cultural control systems in general. A faulty control system will lead to complete lack of cultural balance, an absence of any control system whatsoever is unbounded chaos. Furthermore, calling it a "control system" gets the point across as to what it might result in but doesn't hint strongly enough at the freedom and openness of this type of control. For example, a feeling of shame after an unwholesome act can be part of a cultural control system.

Would it be more fruitful to talk of vernacular control or subtle control systems? A shared conscience, cultural expectations and standards, vectors with a light and delicate controlling power shaping the flow of people's behaviour, almost a vague cultural etiquette? Why must we always be so explicit?
