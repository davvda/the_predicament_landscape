The impossibility of UBI

Strength is paradoxical. I am not strong because I can force others to do what I wish as a result of my play with them, but because I can allow them to do what they wish in the course of my play with them.

- James Carse, Finite & Infinite Games

One of the things that trap humans from doing what their families, local communities, society, and planet need is the obligation to, in order to earn a living, do what corporations and organisations need. Universal basic income could tackle this problem head-on by giving everybody a basic income with no strings attached. However, with the main incentive for nations being economic growth, UBI constantly gets blocked from becoming reality. UBI also requires governments and corporations to trust people to do the right thing, or anything, and since those who tend to rise to the top are ignorant of what the right thing, or anything, is, there will always be colliding incentives here, with beliefs that anyone not forced to work will simply become a criminal, lazy, or, god forbid, content with life.

  • Avoiding uncertainty - Letting people have the freedom to choose what to do with their lives. On that note, I believe a word much more important than labour or work is participation. I do not hold the belief that a lack of labour and work will lead to a lack of societal and social participation. Quite the opposite.
