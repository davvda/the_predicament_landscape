We, as humans, have not evolved for the ways of living modernity offers. Mental health encompasses emotional, psychological, and social well-being, influencing cognition, perception, and behaviour. Mental illness is therefore any long-term negatively affecting emotional or psychological condition which affects cognition, perception, behaviour, and overall well-being. Many mental illnesses are direct adaptations to the life of modernity, ways of managing the dissonance of a life with several needs and wants forever unmet, where some management methods end up being coincidentally beneficial and some crippling. I would even go so far as to say that those who are perfectly mentally healthy in the modern world are more worrying than those who aren't and I would, consequently, quite confidently, say that the cognition, perception, and behaviour required to be truly happy while balls-deep in modernity is the real illness.

Times when I'm struggling, either anxiety, anger, or depression, I have an increased tendency to yank the leash, be completely impatient with any slowing down or messing about. Even mild mental health decline can lead me to decrease the quality of life for one of the animals I love the most in the whole world. If that is a generalisable example, how does our mental health affect how we treat other animals across the earth? I'm sorry, Hilma, I promise that the next walk will be chill and yankless.
