Lack of dark spaces

Work stops at sunset. Darkness falls over the building site. The sky is filled with stars. "There is the blueprint," they say.

- Italo Calvino, Invisible Cities

I wonder how common it is in the history of humans to feel safer at night when the stars aren't visible. Somehow, I sense an obvious issue in that the more we brighten the darkness, the more threatening the darkness becomes, as if we're condensing the beings of darkness, pushing them into the few remaining cramped corners. Unfortunately for us, the less we accept the night, embrace the darkness, the more it seeps into us, corrupts our actions and refracts our rays of thought. Beware the lack of dark spaces, it flatters you not, contorted features, darkened, deepened shadows.

The quality that we call beauty, however, must always grow from the realities of life, and our ancestors, forced to live in dark rooms, presently came to discover beauty in shadows, ultimately to guide shadows towards beauty’s ends.

- Jun'ichirō Tanizaki, In Praise of Shadows

Outgoing connections:

  • Architecture - Much modern architecture abhors darkness and shadows.
  • Biodiversity loss - Light pollution disrupts ecosystems.
  • Cars - Streetlights for cars.
  • Clock time - Diruption of the seasonally dependent day-night cycle.
  • Ecological crisis
  • Energy
  • Hyperstimuli - We tend to speak of hyperstimuli as the more extreme forms of entertainment and such but the amount of lights of different colours and intensities at night in a modern city can be overwhelming for someone not used to it.
  • Ignorance of everything being sacred - Everything blanketed by a star-wisped night sky.
  • Insect loss - Moths to flame.
  • Lack of quiet spaces - Darkness quietens.
  • Madness of safety - One of the main contributors to the madness of safety.
  • Mental health
  • Mistreatment of animals
  • Mistreatment of other humans - The mistreatment of animals is obvious but somehow it feels far-fetched to put the mistreatment of other humans as something connected to the lack of dark spaces. Should it? We're animals too, y'know. What has been disrupted in our natural behaviours due to artificial light?
  • Out of touch with seasons - Why am I not deeply asleep, belly full of pine needles?
  • Over-reliance on technology - I wonder, if ever there are major breakdowns of the electrical system, how long it would take for people everywhere to get lost in the night sky.
  • Spiritual metacrisis
  • The desacration of all places - There is a crypt in my local church that is over 900 years old. Along the entire ceiling of the crypt are strips of LED spotlights aimed at every single dark corner. Even the religious seem to fear the darkness of the divine.
  • The minimisation of all things wild
