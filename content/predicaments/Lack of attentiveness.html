Lack of attentiveness

Being attentive means being observant, seeing changes in one's surroundings, noticing the needs and wants of others, being in service to others. To lack attentiveness is to not notice the effects of one's actions, to not be observant to changes in one's surroundings, unable to tend to that which the world requires from us. Attentiveness is the first step one must take to finally arrive, to get *here*.

Perhaps because it's our deepest desire to discover our true nature, to come to some sense of our oneness with life or to understand who we are or what all this strange thing that we got born into is about. Basically it's the only game in town, if you look at it; everything else is kind of transitory. It is simply to pay attention and discover what the whole process of life and death are about.

- Jack Kornfield
