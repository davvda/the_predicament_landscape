Transportation infrastructure


The movement of people and their goods requires systems of transportation. Besides walking or running, modes of transportation include bikes, horses, animal-drawn carriages, boats, cars, motorcycles, planes, other air vehicles, railway vehicles, and more. These can further be divided into what power is driving them; air, electricity, fossil fuels, muscle, etc. Wheeled vehicles need roads and highways, boats need waterways and ports, airplanes need airports, trains and trams need railways, and people need safe places to walk. Horses just need peaceful retirement. The road to hell is paved with asphalt and the road to asphalt is paved with incentives that ignore the long-term. Asphalt degrades fast, is expensive to change, absorbs heat, contributes to air pollution, and drains badly. Our transportation infrastructure has both caused and become necessary for the global market and, even if we choose to reject globalisation, will keep being a deciding factor in the design of a world suitable to humans.
