Genetic modification

Genetic modification means modifying the underlying genetic blueprint for any organism using technology, usually with the intent of adding desirable traits and/or removing undesirable ones, but also creating novel organisms. It is potentially the final stepping stone in our manipulation-focused mechanistic view of life.

Outgoing connections:

  • Acceleration - Genetic modification is one path to accelerating evolution as well as potentially accelerating us towards catastrophe.
  • Access to food - It might increase crop yields but the total effect of genetic modification on our access to food and proper nutrition is as of yet inconclusive.
  • Access to water - Several genetically modified crops require increased pesticide use, much of which ends up polluting water sources.
  • Agriculture
  • Biodiversity loss - Genetically modified animals and plants are rarely beneficial for their under-developed cousins.
  • Catastrophic risks
  • Complexity mismatch - Genetically modified crops can often mean an increase in yield and a narrowing of ideal conditions.
  • Delusional domination of nature - Even I'll concede that our advancements in genetics is the least delusional domination of nature we've come up with. Though, we should be clear that what we see in the mirror is a demon, not a god.
  • Ecological crisis - The natural systems of Earth are being destabilised by genetically modified organisms.
